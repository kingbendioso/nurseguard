Welcome to NurseGuard

This toolkit empowers nurses with updated tools, education, and forms for the prevention and care of Hospital-Acquired Pressure Injuries (HAPI).

What is HAPI?

Hospital-Acquired Pressure Injuries are localized damage to the skin and underlying tissue, primarily caused by prolonged pressure or shear. Prevention and early intervention are crucial.

Assessment Tools

  • Braden Scale for Predicting Pressure Sore Risk
  • Daily Skin Assessment Protocols
  • Visual Guides to Pressure Injury Stages
  • Red Flags: Non-blanchable erythema, boggy tissue, or temperature change

Management Strategies

  • Reposition every 2 hours and elevate heels
  • Use of pressure-relieving mattresses and cushions
  • Appropriate dressing (e.g., foam, hydrocolloid, alginate) by wound stage
  • Wound cleansing with normal saline or prescribed solution
  • Nutritional support: Protein intake, hydration monitoring
  • Early consult to wound care or tissue viability nurse
  • Education for patient and family on pressure injury care

Reporting & Documentation

Ensure timely and accurate documentation of all pressure injuries.

  • Use SOAP format for detailed nursing notes
  • Take and upload photos (per facility policy)
  • Report using hospital’s EMR or incident system
